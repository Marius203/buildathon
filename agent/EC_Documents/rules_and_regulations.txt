DEFINITIONS
Contactless MasterCard – material element, emitted by a bank or a certified institution, which constitutes a way of collection/payment of goods sold/purchased within the event venue, in conformity with the present TC.

RFID Wristband (RadioFrequency IDentification) – material element that constitutes a way for collection/payment of goods sold/purchased within the event venue.

RFID Wristband Top-up – the process by which participants will change the methods of payment from "cash" to the payment instrument "RFID Wristband” with which they can purchase products sold at the event.

RFID Wristband Money Refund – the inverse process of "RFID Wristband Top-up" and the process by which participants will exchange payment instrument "RFID Wristband" in payment method “cash”. The refunding process of the amount of money implies the initial process of RFID Wristband Top-Up.

The RFID Reader – the device used for the collection/payment of the goods. The amount of money topped up on the RFID Wristband is a multipurpose card voucher (MPV), according to the European Commission Legislation.

The amount loaded on the RFID Wristband is a multi-purpose voucher (MPV), as defined by European Commission legislation.

Festival area – the space marked off by the Organizer consisting of the courtyard surrounding the Banffy Castle and the inside of the Banffy Castle, situated in Bonţida village, Cluj County. Camping area – the space represented by the accommodation area offered by the Organizer, near the festival territory. Access to this space is permitted for participants who have a valid camping pass for the Electric Castle 2026 event. It does not include the festival area.

Indoor area – any area with a roof or ceiling, separated by at least two walls, no matter their structure or nature, temporary or permanent.

Outdoor area – any area which is not considered indoor.

Website – www.electriccastle.com or www.electriccastle.ro

1.5 – Access of people with disabilities to the Electric Castle festival

1.5.1. People with disabilities classified under a degree of disability, as well as their companions, benefit from the following facilities, within the limit of 200 subscriptions for the 4 days of the festival (a limit imposed considering the total capacity of the Festival, the capacity of the accessible areas, as well as safety and security regulations):

Children (minors under 18 years of age) with disabilities, regardless of the degree of disability (severe, accentuated, medium, or mild), and one companion – benefit free of charge from a subscription for the 4 days of the festival of the General Access Pass type (without camping).

Adults with severe or accentuated disabilities and one companion – benefit free of charge from a subscription for the 4 days of the festival of the General Access Pass type (without camping).

Considering that these facilities can only be granted within the limit of a maximum number of 200 places, taking into account the total capacity of the Festival, the capacity of the accessible areas, as well as safety and security regulations, free access will be granted based on the order in which requests are received.

If the number of 200 requests that meet the conditions specified in these Regulations is reached, the Organizer will indicate in these Regulations that the maximum number of subscriptions granted free of charge to people with disabilities has been reached, and from that moment on, the Organizer will no longer respond to requests addressed for this purpose.

The companion of an adult with a medium or mild disability pays the full price. By exception, if the need for a companion has been established by an evaluation commission and can be proven by a document issued by a competent authority or has been recorded in the disability degree certificate, the companion of an adult with a medium or mild disability will also benefit from the same discount as the adult with a medium or mild disability whom they accompany.

1.5.3. Conditions for granting the facilities mentioned in points 1.5.1 and 1.5.2:
The facilities are granted based on the following valid documents, presented for verification:

Disability degree certificate (issued by competent authorities);

Identity document;

Optional, the European Disability Card.
